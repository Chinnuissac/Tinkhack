What to Eat During Postpartum

A postpartum eating plan should include a balance of food groups with a focus on minimally processed foods:

  • Whole grains like brown rice, whole wheat pasta, and barley.
  • Lean proteins such as beans, lentils, poultry, and seafood.
  • Healthy fats, like nuts, peanut butter, avocados, and olive oil.
  • Fruits, including berries, bananas, and melon.
  • Vegetables, such as dark leafy greens, bell peppers, carrots, and broccoli.

Focus on regular meals and snacks to stabilize energy levels while dealing with the disruptions of postpartum life.

Postpartum Weight Management

Managing weight postpartum can be challenging due to factors like poor sleep, lack of physical activity, and postpartum depression. It's important to nourish your body with balanced meals rather than resorting to crash diets.

Consider using the plate method for weight management: ¼ plate of whole grains, ¼ plate of lean protein, and ½ plate of non-starchy vegetables. Stay active with stroller walks or parent-baby yoga classes, and give yourself grace during this transition.

Meal Plan for Postpartum

Here’s a 5-day meal plan to help nourish you during the postpartum period. The meals are easy to prepare and nutritious. Each day offers variety, but leftovers are highly recommended for efficiency.

Day 1

Breakfast: Slow cooker apple cinnamon steel cut oatmeal

Lunch: Salad with quinoa, mandarin oranges, rotisserie chicken, and sesame dressing

Dinner: Slow cooker chicken tortilla soup

Snacks: Peanut butter protein bars, smoothie with Greek yogurt and fruit

Day 2

Breakfast: Chia seed pudding with coconut milk, raspberries, and cacao nibs

Lunch: Mediterranean pasta salad with whole wheat pasta, cherry tomatoes, and feta cheese

Dinner: Stuffed pepper casserole with ground turkey and brown rice

Snacks: Greek yogurt blueberry muffins, trail mix with nuts

Tips for Meal Preparation

Meal prep can be difficult when you're caring for a newborn. Try batch-cooking, freezing meals, and using slow cookers or instant pots. Accept help whenever possible to ensure you can nourish yourself while recovering from childbirth.
